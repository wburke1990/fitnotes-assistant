Exercise ideas

  Exercises to add/emphasize for Pes Anserine:
  - Isometric Wall Sit - VMO strengthening, no shear force
  - Terminal Knee Extensions (TKE) - VMO focus, band resistance
  - Side-Lying Hip Adduction - Gentle adductor work
  - Hamstring Curls - Already have Monkey Foot Curls ✓
  - Clamshells - Hip external rotation, prevents valgus

Phase 1 — Rebuild (Weeks 1–4)
1. Short Arc Quads (SAQ)

3×20 with 2–3 sec VMO squeeze

2. Adductor Long-Lever Isometrics

1–2 minute holds each leg (side-lying leg lifts or Copenhagen regressions)

3. Sartorius/Gracilis Activation (“Cross-Leg Raise”)

Sit with legs straight, cross one ankle over the other, raise bottom leg upward
2×15 each leg
(Great for medial dynamic support)

4. Glute Med Kickstand RDLs

Use kickstand stance with strict pelvis control
3×12 light–moderate
(This builds real anti-rotation strength)


Phase 2 — Stabilize Under Load (Weeks 4–8)
1. Split-squat isometric holds at midrange

Hold 20–30 sec each side
Build to holding load in goblet position

2. Banded Knee Valgus Resistance

Mini-band above the knee while squatting to teach active valgus control
3×12

3. Copenhagen Plank (Short then Long lever)

Hold 10–20 sec × 3

4. Hamstring Curls with Tibial Internal Rotation

Toes slightly inward
3×12–15


Phase 3 — Strength at Fatigue (Weeks 8–12)

This is where your injury will never recur once complete.

1. Lateral Step-Downs (controlled, slow)

3×8–10
Focus on knee track

2. Heavy Split Squats with Neutral Tibia

Load only if:

Knee tracks cleanly

No pes-anserine burn

No medial wobble

3. Rotational RDLs

(Think Mahler-style anti-rotation hinge; not twisting, resisting twist)
3×5 per leg

4. Finishers ONLY with anti-rotation goals

Examples:

Pallof press holds

Offset farmer carries

Banded tibial rotations (isometrics)


A. Barbell Glute Bridge (not hip thrust)

This is stronger, safer, and requires far less knee rotation control.

Why it’s ideal for YOU:

Tibia stays vertical = minimal rotational demand

Load goes straight to the glutes

Medial knee structures basically get a break

You can load this stupidly heavy (405–585 eventually) safely.

Programming:
4×8–12 heavy
or
3×20 moderate

D. Weighted Kickstand RDL (Glute-biased)

Leave 90% of the load on front leg
Back foot is only a balance kickstand
Absolutely crushes glutes without rotation.
